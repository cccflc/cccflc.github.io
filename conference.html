SESSION 1 - 10:00 AM
"LibreTexts and H5P: How We Created a Spanish OER Grammar Manual"
Cristina Moon, Ph.D., Chabot College
Alejandro Lee Ph.D., Santa Monica College
Nancy Meléndez-Ballesteros, C. Phil., Santa Monica College and Mount Saint Mary’s University-Los Angeles

Community college students spend up to $1,000 in textbooks and online access codes to complete 4 levels of Spanish. The rising costs affect their engagement and success in the classroom. Thus, we wanted to provide open-access Spanish content created with our student population in mind.

Entrada Libre is an intermediate/advanced college-level Spanish OER grammar manual for second language learners and heritage learners. The manual along with the H5P activities can be downloaded or remixed in LibreTexts, and uploaded to your LMS. It can be used with Acceso (an OER from the University of Kansas).

SESSION 2 - 1:00 PM
"Artificial Intelligence in the Language Classroom"
Dr. Fabián Banga, Berkeley City College
ASL interpretation to be provided.

This session considers the impact of artificial intelligence in college classrooms, especially for those teaching languages. Advances have led to the development of powerful and relatively accurate translation and speech recognition tools. With such technology, what utility or incentive is there for learning a language? What are the opportunities/issues like AI translations and speech recognition capabilities to convert languages to text? This session addresses these technologies and how they challenge and benefit language learning.

Zoom link: https://cccd-edu.zoom.us/meeting/register/tJ0qcO2prTssHd18WQyz4m-upk6MEry721Ru

SESSION 3 - 3:00 PM
"Canvas: Best Practices for Teaching Synchronously and Asynchronously"
Adrienne Merlo, Adjunct Spanish Professor for Orange Coast College and Cypress College
Click here for presentation outline
ASL interpretation to be provided.

Since I began using Canvas in 2016, my skills in designing and utilizing this LMS as an effective tool for teaching Spanish have increased tenfold. Initially, I used it solely to post documents, announcements and for grading (according to FERPA). However, after being thrown into the deep end of the online teaching pool in 2020, I rapidly became a fast swimmer.

I had serendipitously enrolled in an online course through @one in February of 2020 and after 12-weeks (approximately 120 hours), I earned my Certificate for Online Teaching & Design. I now use Canvas extensively to teach Beginning Spanish online via Zoom as well as Intermediate Spanish asynchronously.

During my presentation, I intend to share my best practices for designing Canvas course sites, specifically as a comparison/contrast of using it to teach synchronously versus asynchronously. In return, I hope to learn a few new tricks from all of you during the discussion immediately following.
